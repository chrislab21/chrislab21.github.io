About

Meet the Artist

It all began when I played Majora's Mask as a young kid. I was always in awe of the way that artists and animators could use such low-poly models, but animate them and bring them to life in such a way that we could all understand the emotion behind every movement. Until that point, I always thought I would grow up to be a writer, but the art of video games continued to inspire me into my adult life, when I decided I wanted to be a storyteller of a different kind.

Telling stories has always been a way for me to connect to people. It allows us to see into the lives of others, and put ourselves into the shoes of someone else for a while, and empathize with their experiences. I aspire to tell stories that really affect people. To put them into a position where they can grapple with and understand the struggles and joys of others that they might otherwise never experience.
